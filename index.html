DrugMatch Beginner Project

// Drug repurposing explorer

Finding new cures
in existing drugs

This system scans a small set of known drugs and diseases, then looks for hidden connections through shared biological targets — the same core idea behind EveryCure's AI platform.

// 01 — how it works

Three dictionaries, one big idea

The system stores three types of facts. Then it looks for overlap. When a drug and a disease share a biological target, a potential match is flagged.

Step 01

💊

Store what each drug already treats

Metformin treats type 2 diabetes. Aspirin treats heart disease and pain. These are the known uses.

Step 02

🔬

Map drugs and diseases to biological targets

Metformin affects AMPK and mTOR. Alzheimer's also involves mTOR. That shared target is the clue.

Step 03

✦

Score and rank every match

More shared targets = higher score = stronger repurposing hypothesis. The best leads rise to the top.

// 03 — the big picture

Why this matters

Drugs already on shelves could be treating diseases we haven't tried yet.

Developing a brand new drug takes over a decade and costs billions of dollars. Most of that time is spent proving the drug is safe. But drugs already approved by the FDA have already passed safety testing.

If an approved drug shares a biological target with a disease it was never designed for, it might work — and it could reach patients years faster than a new drug would.

This tiny Python system demonstrates that idea in about 50 lines of beginner code. The real EveryCure platform does the same thing across 4,000 drugs and 18,000 diseases — 75 million possible matches — using AI to score every single one.
